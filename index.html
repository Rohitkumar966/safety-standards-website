Factories Act, 1948 (Updated)

The Factories Act is India's primary legislation for ensuring occupational safety in industrial facilities. It covers aspects like health, safety, welfare, and working hours of workers in factories.

  • Section 41B: Safety management system
  • Section 87: Dangerous operations
  • Section 111A: Right of workers to be informed about hazards

ISO Safety Standards

Some of the important ISO standards followed in Indian industries:

  • ISO 45001: Occupational Health and Safety Management Systems
  • ISO 14001: Environmental Management Systems
  • ISO 31000: Risk Management Guidelines

OSHA Guidelines (International)

Though not legally binding in India, OSHA standards are referenced for best practices in high-risk sectors.

  • 29 CFR 1910: General Industry Standards
  • OSHA Hazard Communication Standard

BIS Standards (Bureau of Indian Standards)

BIS publishes safety-related codes and norms for machinery, PPE, fire equipment, etc.

  • IS 14489: Occupational Safety and Health Audit
  • IS 2925: Safety Helmets
  • IS 15298: Safety Footwear

National Building Code (NBC) Safety Norms

The NBC outlines fire and structural safety requirements for industrial and commercial buildings in India.

  • Part 4: Fire and Life Safety
  • Part 7: Constructional Practices and Safety
